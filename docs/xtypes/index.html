X-renderers

Any X-tag declared and defined in a project always invokes a so-called "X-renderer":

  • a specific, specialized programmatic output-rendering routine integrated into the HaXtatic code-base,
  • each offering its own degree of configurability (the once-per-tag-definition settings) and parameterization (the varying-per-tag-invocation settings).

HaXtatic ships with numerous such simple-yet-versatile X-renderers that are listed below, and custom ones may be coded up and integrated straightforwardly in Haskell (if minimally comfortable with the language and its tool-chain) — starting from the 2 demo* starter samples.

All the following built-in X-renderers are ready to use in X-tag definitions:

  • hax.miniTag
    Outputs <somehtmltag ..>tag's inner-markup content</somehtmltag>.
  • hax.image
    Outputs <img .. /> or <a ..><img .. /></a>.
  • hax.xmlEscape
    Outputs the given content with all occurrences of any of & < > " ' each appropriately entity-escaped for safe inclusion in markup.
  • hax.link
    Outputs <a ..>..</a>.
  • hax.links
    Outputs a sequence of <a ..> .. </a> or <outerhtmltag ..><a ..> .. </a></outerhtmltag> in a uniform manner.
  • hax.pageAnchors
    Outputs a sequence of <outerhtmltag ..><a ..> .. </a></outerhtmltag>, one per <gatherhtmltag> element found in the page's content source.
  • hax.snippet
    Outputs the content of the named snippet with the specified variable substitutions.
  • hax.repeat
    Outputs a sequence of contents enumerated by the specified iterator.
  • hax.demoSimplest
    Outputs <h1>Hello World!</h1>.
  • hax.demoCfgArgs
    Outputs <h1>Hello, greetname!</h1>.
