X-tags: dynamic renderers

An {X| .. |} tag invokes a programmatic output renderer (aka. {X|lx:index X-renderer|}).

Such an "X-renderer" may be of a built-in type, or of a custom one developed in Haskell and linked into the {T|Hax|} code-base manually. Naturally only the built-in types are covered here and in the {X|lx:index {B|title:xtypes|}|} section.

Like T-tags (and unlike P- and B-tags), there are no predefined X-tags, only those {X|lb:haxproj#xdecl declared and set up in *.haxproj files|} — ie. before an X-tag can be embedded many times anywhere in the project, it is first to be defined once in a *.haxproj: to be given a name and some default settings.

Here are the built-in X-renderers ready to set up and try out:

  • demoSimplest — "Hello World!"
  • demoCfgArgs — "Hello, foo!"
  • hax.miniTag — eliminate verbosity of repetetive markup
  • hax.htmlLink — less-verbose hyperlinks
  • hax.htmlImage — less-verbose images
  • hax.htmlLinks — lean & handy navigation helper
  • hax.htmlAnchors — gather and link to in-page contents
  • hax.xmlEscape — HTML/XML/etc.-escape given content
  • hax.dtFormat — dates formatted as desired
  • hax.snippet — elsewhere known as "custom controls", "components", "sub-templates", or "thinglets"..
  • hax.iterator — versatile content/record selector→rearranger→reformatter→renderer
