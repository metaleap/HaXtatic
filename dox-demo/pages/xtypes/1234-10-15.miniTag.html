{X|haxdox-xthead|} {T|Xearly|} {T|Xpurpose|}

Some contents require repetetive use of quickly-becoming-too-verbose "mostly just inner text" tags such as {X|c: <blockquote> |}, {X|c: <code> |}, or {X|c: <p class="my-para-highlighted my-smallerfont"> |} etc.

Setting up hax.miniTag X-tags for any such instances alleviates this quickly, and lets the author turn repetetive-hence-too-verbose occurrences of:

  • {X|c: <blockquote>content</blockquote> |}
    → into {X|c: {X|bq:content|} |}
  • {X|c: <code>content</code> |}
    → into {X|c: {X|c:content|} |}
  • {X|c: <p class="my-para-highlighted my-smallerfont my-roundborder">content</p> |}
    → into {X|c: {X|phs:content|} |}
  • et cetera..
{T|Xcfg|}

To demonstrate valid {X|lb:haxproj#xdecl *.haxproj|} directives to declare the above 3 example {X|lt:x X-tags|}:

|X|hax.miniTag:bq: blockquote

|X|hax.miniTag:c:
    code: attr = []

|X|hax.miniTag:phs: p: attr
    = [ ("class" , "my-para-highlighted my-smallerfont"),
        ("class" , "my-roundborder") ]

To elaborate, for example the last one of the above, as all {X|lb:haxproj#xdecl |X| directives|} declaring {X|lt:x X-tags|} do:

  • begins with {X|c: |X| |} followed by the X-renderer identifier (here {X|c:hax.miniTag|})
  • followed by {X|c: :|} colon and the desired X-tag name to be used to invoke it (here {X|c:phs|}),
  • followed by {X|c: :|} colon and now hax.miniTag-specific configuration:
    • The name of the output markup-language tag that this X-tag should wrap its content in (here {X|c:blockquote|}),
    • followed by a {X|c: :|} colon if an optional {X|lb:syntax syntax-sensitive|} properties block follows, comprised of all of the following properties in this very order:
      1. {X|c:attr = [ .. ]|} — a List of all the tag attributes (each denoted in a {X|c:("name","value")|} pair) to always append to the markup-language tag output by all instances of this X-tag
        • The special attribute value {X|c: {%:content:%}|} will replicate the X-tag content inside this attribute (not specially escaped — effect this by wrapping inside a {X|lt:p#delay page-delayed|} X-tag invoking {X|lx:xmlEscape hax.xmlEscape|})
{T|Xargs|}

No special tag parameters, no special content treatment: the entire X-tag content (ie. following the tag name such as above {X|c:phs|} and then {X|c: :|} colon, as in {X|c:{X|phs:content|}|}) is output as-is, inside the markup-language tag configured for the X-tag.

Caution: if no such X-tag content is present, nothing is rendered out at all, not even the intended (would-be empty) markup-language tag. This is by design and more often than not desirable especially in certain scenarios of somewhat-dynamic tag contents.
