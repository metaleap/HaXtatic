Syntactic rules (in places)

A number of certain directives (placed in {X|lb:haxproj *.haxproj|} files or implied in {X|lb:tmplsnip#snippets *.haxsnip.html|}s' headers) and haXtags (placed anywhere) — specifically:

  • all {X|lb:haxproj#bdecl |B| directives|} (following the Blok name)
  • all {X|lb:haxproj#pdecl |P| directives|} (following the feed name)
  • the {X|lb:haxproj#cdecl |C| directives|} {X|c:process:static:|} and {X|c:process:pages:|}
  • most {X|lb:haxproj#xdecl |X| directives|} (except the 2 {X|lt:x#demo demo|} X-renderers for coders)
  • some {X|lt:x X-tags|} (determined and documented by the {X|lx:index X-renderer|} invoked)

— absolutely require {X|note: —lest they abort the entire process abruptly (with {X|c:|C|:_hax_onparseerror:|} {X|lb:haxproj#cmisc set to|} {X|c:abort|}) or render warning/error messages (otherwise) into generated output contents— |} strict adherence to the following (essentially trivial, still currently rather demanding-and-unforgiving thanks to the.. "compactness" of the parser) syntax rules:

  • All the properties shown need to appear,
    • each separated from the next by a {X|c: ,|} comma,
    • and in the very same order showing in documentation!
  • A text value is enclosed in {X|c: "|} quotation marks:
    • {X|c:= "Wrote this in \"VBScript\" back in '99.."|}
    • Inside literal line-breaks are supported; all standard escape sequences work predictably, eg. {X|c: \"|} for in-text quotation marks etc.)
    • Any {X|lt:index haXtags|} placed inside such quoted text-values (principally those that might output quotation marks eg. HTML attributes) should escape the entire tag content (everything in between {X|c: {*||} and {X|c: |}|}) by prefixing it with 2 {X|c: `|} backticks:
      • ie. by changing {X|c:{T|sometextname|}|}, {X|c:{P|1st:div|}|} or {X|c:{X|myctl:|}|}
        into {X|c:{T|``sometextname|}|}, {X|c:{P|``1st:div|}|} or {X|c:{X|``myctl:|}|} respectively
  • A tuple is a name-value pair enclosed within {X|c:( )|} parentheses: two text-values associated together (such as an URL and its caption, or an attribute's name and its value), separated by a {X|c: ,|} comma:
    • {X|c:wrapEach = ("prefix here" , "suffix here")|}
  • A list is enclosed by {X|c: [|} and {X|c: ]|} and may contain any number of either text values (each separated from the next by a {X|c: ,|} comma), or tuples (dito), or nothing at all:
    • {X|c: more = ["customfield1" , "customfield2" , "customfieldX"] |}
    • {X|c: more = [] |}
    • {X|c: htmlAtts = [ ("class","pagelink") , ("href","{P|/blog/index.html|}") ] |}
  • Other values (numbers, yes/no toggles, optionals and more) are explicitly documented separately for each property on the approriate topic page.
  • Text-value properties shown in documentation without enclosing {X|c: "|} quotation marks and with a {X|c: =>|} connector (in place of the usual {X|c: =|}) are in each and every instance
    • the only text-value property in the directive/haXtag that may omit the enclosing quotation marks (and that then simply extends until the very end of the directive/haXtag)
    • but only if that property's name is followed immediately (no spaces) by exactly {X|c:=>|} in place of the usual {X|c: =|} assignment operator.
    • No {X|c: \"|} escaping of quotation marks required in here, also no {X|lt:index haXtags|} in here require the above-mentioned {X|c: ``|} double-backtick escaping.
    • Such properties may however, if somehow desired, also be written the same way as any other text value, that is with {X|c: = ".."|} plus any required inner escaping.
